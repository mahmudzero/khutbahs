# The first half

Our conversation today will be about the importance of (righteous) companionship, and it's proof in
the Quran and Sunnah.

## Allah SWT says in Surah Ali Imran, verse 103

﴿وَاعتَصِموا بِحَبلِ اللَّهِ جَميعًا وَلا تَفَرَّقوا وَاذكُروا نِعمَتَ اللَّهِ عَلَيكُم إِذ كُنتُم أَعداءً فَأَلَّفَ بَينَ قُلوبِكُم فَأَصبَحتُم بِنِعمَتِهِ إِخوانًا وَكُنتُم عَلى شَفا حُفرَةٍ مِنَ النّارِ فَأَنقَذَكُم مِنها كَذلِكَ يُبَيِّنُ اللَّهُ لَكُم آياتِهِ لَعَلَّكُم تَهتَدونَ﴾

﴾ And hold on to Allah's rope all together, and do not split. And remember Allahs bounty on you, when you were enemies and He softened between your hearts, so that you became brothers through His bounty. You were on the edge of Hell Fire and He saved you. This is how Allah shows you his signs, so that you may attain enlightenment. ﴿

## Allah SWT also says in Surah Al Fath, verse 29

﴿مُحَمَّدٌ رَسولُ اللَّهِ وَالَّذينَ مَعَهُ أَشِدّاءُ عَلَى الكُفّارِ رُحَماءُ بَينَهُم تَراهُم رُكَّعًا سُجَّدًا يَبتَغونَ فَضلًا مِنَ اللَّهِ وَرِضوانًا سيماهُم في وُجوهِهِم مِن أَثَرِ السُّجودِ ذلِكَ مَثَلُهُم فِي التَّوراةِ وَمَثَلُهُم فِي الإِنجيلِ كَزَرعٍ أَخرَجَ شَطأَهُ فَآزَرَهُ فَاستَغلَظَ فَاستَوى عَلى سوقِهِ يُعجِبُ الزُّرّاعَ لِيَغيظَ بِهِمُ الكُفّارَ وَعَدَ اللَّهُ الَّذينَ آمَنوا وَعَمِلُوا الصّالِحاتِ مِنهُم مَغفِرَةً وَأَجرًا عَظيمًا﴾

﴾ Mohammed SAWS is the Messenger of Allah, and His companions are strict with the non-believers and merciful between each other. You see them in rukuu' and sujood, seeking Allah's bounty and pleasure...

Their sign is in their faces from the effects of sujood. That is their description in the Torah. Their description in the Injeel (Gospels) is like a plant that has given its offshoots, and it strengthens them so they grow firm and stand on their stalks, delighting the sowers. So that Allah enrages the disbelivers by the belivers.

Allah has promised the ones who have believed and done righteous deeds forgiveness and great reward. ﴿

## Allah SWT also says in Surah Ali Imran, verse 28

﴿لا يَتَّخِذِ المُؤمِنونَ الكافِرينَ أَولِياءَ مِن دونِ المُؤمِنينَ وَمَن يَفعَل ذلِكَ فَلَيسَ مِنَ اللَّهِ في شَيءٍ إِلّا أَن تَتَّقوا مِنهُم تُقاةً وَيُحَذِّرُكُمُ اللَّهُ نَفسَهُ وَإِلَى اللَّهِ المَصيرُ﴾

The Believers should not take the disbelievers as allies in place of the Believers. Whoever does this has nothing with Allah, except when they are doing this as a form of protection. And Allah warns you of Himself, and to Allah is the (final) destination.

# The second half

بسم الله، والحمدلله، والصلاة والسلام على رسول الله، وعلى اله وصحبه ومن والاه

We've seen that the Quran and Sunnah highlights the importance of righteous and Muslim companionship.

In Ali Imran, Allah SWT ties salvation from Hell Fire to Islamic Brotherhood. We saw this in verse 103.

In Surah Al Fath, Allah SWT says that the people with The Rasool SAWS are merciful and gentle amongst themselves. Who are these people? The Sahaba RA, the people who Allah in the Quran says He is pleased with them, and they are pleased with him. We saw this in the last verse, verse 109.

How does Islam say to nurture, and enforce these relationships amongst our selves? The Quran, Sunnah, and lives of the Sahaba and Salaf also gives us a prescription.

Lets start by looking at a very basic way of doing this, and then we will build up,

## Allah SWT says in Surah Al Nisa, verse 86

﴿وَإِذا حُيّيتُم بِتَحِيَّةٍ فَحَيّوا بِأَحسَنَ مِنها أَو رُدّوها إِنَّ اللَّهَ كانَ عَلى كُلِّ شَيءٍ حَسيبًا﴾

And if you are given a greeting then greet with a greater one, or return it. Indeed, Allah holds account all things.

Ibn Katheer and others have said that this means, when someone says salam to you, you should return a higher version of the salam, or at the very least return an identical salam. What does this mean, well we know there are levels to the salam,
- السلام عليكم
- السلام عليكم ورحمة الله
- السلام عليكم ورحمة الله وبركاته

So if someone says \<the first one\>, then respond with it, or one of the two higher ones. And Allah SWT is telling us that even of this seemingly simple matter between us, He will hold us accountable.

On the authority of Abu Huraira RA, he said "I asked the Rasool SAWS, tell me of a matter that if I follow it, I will enter Jannah with it", the Rassol SAWS said "Spread the salam, and feed people food, and keep the ties of kinship, and pray while the people sleep, you will enter Jannah with peace"

This hadith is very dense, and I've only given part of it, and I want to focus in on a smaller part. The Rasool SAWS gave Abu Huraria RA 4 deeds to commit to and promised him Paradise by keeping those 4 deeds, __3__ of the deeds have to do with our interactions between each other.

## Also in the Masnad of Imam Ahmad, we see the story of Salman Al Farisi

Specifically, the part of his story where he purchases his freedom. While Salman RA became who he became in terms of rank amongst the Sahaba, at the time that he was purchasing his freedom he was still a new Muslim, new to the companionship. He wasn't like Abu Bakr, or Ali, or Omar RA, as a slave he couldn't even partake in Badr or Uhud, his days were spent in service of his owner, so he wasn't known by everyone. But when the Rasool SAWS announced to the Sahaba RA that Salman RA needed help purchasing his freedom, everyone pitched in. Not because they've known him for years, but purely because he was a Muslim who needed help.

## Lets look at stories from our Righteous Predecessors that also increase the love among Muslims and strengthen our ties.

During the wars against the Romans and the Persians, we hear a beautiful story about Ikrimah ibn Abi Jahl RA (mention his story, tooth and nail). He was fataly wounded on the battle field during the Battle of Yarmouk, when one of the soldiers saw him he offered him water, but Ikrimah RA saw another Muslim that was also wounded so he instructed the person offering him water to give it to the other Muslim. The Muslims on the battle field kept deferring the water to the next person they saw that they wanted to save until Allah SWTs decree was fulfilled, and they all died as Martyrs.

## The last duty I want to talk about has become the hardest to uphold in the modern world,

The Rasool SAWS says in a widely narrated Hadith,

 عَنْ  تَمِيمٍ الدَّارِيِّ ، أَنَّ النَّبِيَّ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ قَالَ : " الدِّينُ النَّصِيحَةُ ". قُلْنَا : لِمَنْ ؟ قَالَ : " لِلَّهِ وَلِكِتَابِهِ وَلِرَسُولِهِ وَلِأَئِمَّةِ الْمُسْلِمِينَ وَعَامَّتِهِمْ ".

On the authority of Tameem RA, the Rasool SAWS said "The religion is the reminder", the Sahaba said "For whom?", the Rasool SAWS Responded "For Allah and His Book and His Messanger and the leaders of the Muslims and the laymen"

This Hadith is very powerful, and it is one of the greatest duties a Muslim has on another Muslim. I started off our talk today with a Hadith where the Rasool SAWS says that a person is on the belief of their close friend, that Hadith and this one go hand in hand. It is obligatory on us to give reminders to each other, Islam is built on enforcing what is right and forbiding what is wrong, with that comes Nasee7a (reminder) among friends. Part of the mercy a Muslim can have on his friend is to call them out, kindly and in private, when they see them doing something wrong and helping guide them back to the right path.

I need to put this caveat here, as sadly this has become common. Anyone can give a reminder, the worst of us and the best of us, a reminder from a Muslim to another Muslim does not mean person X is a better person than person Y because X reminded Y, nor does it mean X is guaranteed Heaven and Y is not. We are all sinners, we all need reminders.

Brothers and Sisters, we need to keep the ties between us, start on a grass roots level of having rahma between us, and pushing each other to perfecting our Deen. And ان شاء الله we will be given victory, peace, and stability once more.
